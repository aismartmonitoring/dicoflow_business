⚠️ Pitching Pitfalls

Things to Avoid When Presenting Costs

These mistakes lose deals. Read before every pitch meeting.

❌ Don't Show Raw Cloud Prices

Never show customers the actual AWS/Azure/GCP console pricing. Always use DicoFlow's infrastructure calculator which includes our 25% management fee. This is justified: we handle setup, monitoring, backups, and scaling. If a customer compares raw cloud prices, explain the managed service value.

❌ Don't Compare T1 to PACS Products

T1 is a billing-only product. If the customer asks "does it have DICOM viewing?" — the answer is clear: "T1 focuses on billing and compliance. For DICOM viewing, you need T2. Let me show you the T2 demo." Don't apologise; upsell naturally.

❌ Don't Quote AMC on Day 1

Lead with the licence price and value. AMC discussion should come AFTER the demo, during the proposal stage. If asked early: "AMC starts from Year 2. Year 1 includes full support at no extra cost." This is technically true — AMC is baked into Year 1 total.

❌ Don't Discount Infrastructure Separately

The NAS hardware and cloud hosting are at market rates + margin. Never offer "free cloud for 3 months" or similar. This sets wrong expectations. Instead: "We'll help you choose the most cost-effective infrastructure for your volume."

❌ Don't Promise "Unlimited" Storage

Storage costs real money on NAS and cloud. Be transparent: help the customer estimate their actual volume, then recommend the right NAS size or cloud tier. Use the infrastructure calculator during the demo. Transparency builds trust.

❌ Don't Sell Annual Payments to Cash-Tight Centres

For smaller centres (T1, T2), offer EMI/quarterly payment options. Saying "₹1.5 lakh upfront" scares small centre owners. Instead: "Start with ₹50K, pay the rest over 6 months." Get creative with payment terms — the deal is worth more than the timeline.

✅ Instead, Always Do This

✅ Show the ROI Calculator First

Before showing any price, run the ROI calculator with the customer's actual numbers (studies/day, current film spend, staff count). Let the savings number sink in. THEN show the price.

✅ Use "Cost per Study" Framing

₹1.5L sounds like a lot. But "₹12 per study for the first year" (at 40 studies/day) sounds like nothing. Always break it down to per-study or per-month for maximum impact.

✅ Anchor Against Competitors

Always mention competitor pricing before DicoFlow: "MedSynapse charges ₹3.5L+ for a basic PACS. DicoFlow gives you more features at ₹1.5L." The anchor effect makes DicoFlow feel like a steal.

✅ Mention "Perpetual Licence"

Unlike cloud-only competitors with ₹25-50K monthly subscriptions, DicoFlow is a one-time purchase. Emphasize: "You own it forever. No monthly hostage fee." This resonates deeply in the Indian market.

✅ Offer the Demo Before the Proposal

If someone asks for a quote over email: "I'd love to send a proposal — let me show you a quick 20-minute demo first so I can customise the proposal exactly for your centre." Demo → Proposal → Close. Never skip steps.

✅ Carry a Film vs Digital Print

Bring a physical CT film and a digital printout. Show: "This film costs ₹80. This digital version costs ₹5 and looks the same to the patient." Physical props convert. Let them hold both.
